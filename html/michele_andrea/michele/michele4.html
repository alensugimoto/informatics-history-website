ARPANET map

ARPANET was very small in his launch in 1969. It also was a closed network until 1979, were only defense researchers could exchange information. It later opened to other types of communication, including email (which wasn’t present in the beginning), and transitoned to the structure of the modern internet in later years, opening to the public.

ARPAnet Sourcebook

The ARPAnet Sourcebook: The Unpublished Foundations of the Internet is a book clarifies the development, the structure, and the outcomes that ARPANET had on the advancement of the World Wide Web. It's a logical examination about what is seemingly the first long-range network to interconnect PCs.
