Ray Tomlinson
Raymond Samuel Tomlinson, born on April 23, 1941, was an American computer programmer who implemented the first email program on the ARPANET system in 1971. He died on March 5, 2016 because of a heart attack. He is internationally known and credited as the inventor of email. He invented the first file system that enabled people to send mail between users that were on different hosts, but connected to ARPANET. Prior to this, email could only be sent to users that used the same machine. To achieve this concept of people sending emails not necessarily to the same machine they use, he used the @ sign to separate the user name from the name of their machine. This scheme continues being used in email addresses ever since.
