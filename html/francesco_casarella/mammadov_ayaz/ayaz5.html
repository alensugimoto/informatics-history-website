International Bodies of Standardization

Internet Engineering Task Force (IETF)

This is an institute which develops and promotes Internet Standards, which are the standards for dealing with devices over a network, in particular it is most known for the standards that comprise the Internet Protocol Suite (TCP/UDP). This standardization is very important because if we did not agree upon a certain protocol then it would be almost impossible to communicated with other devices over a network.

Internet Engineering Task Force (IETF)

This is an institute which develops and promotes Internet Standards, which are the standards for dealing with devices over a network, in particular it is most known for the standards that comprise the Internet Protocol Suite (TCP/UDP). There is no formal membership, meaning that anyone can contribute, new ideas and standards are agreed up by a Rough Consensus (this cannot really be formally defined, it is defined by a general agreement of a group). This standardization is very important because if we did not agree upon a certain protocol then it would be almost impossible to communicated with other devices over a network.

Institute of Electrical and Electronic Engineers (IEEE)

Similar to the IETF, the IEEE is responsible for standards that govern a much lower level of things such, much closer to the hardware. An important example would be wired/wireless networks, and the agreed upon specification of how devices use said standards to interact with each other. Different to the IETF though.

Institute of Electrical and Electronic Engineers (IEEE)

Similar to the IETF, the IEEE is responsible for standards that govern a much lower level of things such, much closer to the hardware. An important example would be wired/wireless networks, and the agreed upon specification of how devices use said standards to interact with each other. Different to the IETF though, in order to be a member one has to fulfill certain academic or professional criteria, and decisions are agreed upon by the consensus of 20 or so Technical committees. As of 2018 the IEEE has about 423,000 members

Internation Organization for Standardization (ISO)

This institute outdates most of Computer Science, being established in 1920, its main purpose is to facilitate standards between nations to render trade simple and easier to carry out. It is comprised of 164 members from a variety of nations, relevant to Computer Science, are the technical committees.
