”Satoshi Nakamoto” And The Bitcoin System

In 2007 an anonymous person or group under the pseudonym ”Satoshi Nakamoto” began working on the writing of the code for bitcoin.

On 18 August 2008 a website under the domain bitcoin.org was created.

On 31 October 2008, he published a paper describing a digital cryptocurrency, titled ”Bitcoin: A Peer-to-Peer Electronic Cash System”. The goal of this document was to describe the innerworkings of this new cash system, and particularly how it would operate and be kept secure.


To summarize his work briefly, and give some context, the bitcoin cash system is based on a new type of database that is mantained by all the member in his network, called a blockchain database, and whose task is to keep track of a single history of all Bitcoin transactions. To provide redundancy thus to ensure that the data won't be lost or changed, but also to allow anyone to verify transaction by themself, the database is publicly avaiable for anyone to view, inspect and download.

The sequences of transactions are stored inside a block in the database, each block is identified by an incrementing number and a unique Sha-256 hash which is calculated by using the transactions stored in the block and the previous block's hash. This procedure "links" every block to his predecessors, thus creating the block-chain (the only exception is the genesis block, the only block which is hard-coded in the initial program).



To secure the data in the block, keep all members of the network in sync (database included), and prevent double spending (spending money that someone doesn't have), a cryptographic algorithm called the proof-of-work is used. this algorithm is also used to generate new block for the database, a procedure known as "Mining" and the reward for doing so is new generated bitcoins, which are given to the miner. This is also the only way to create new bitcoins. (Anyone in the network can be a miner, a miner is simply a computer that uses his computational power to create the new block thus helping processing and securing transactions).



The Bitcoin system thus allow online payments to be sent directly from one party to another without going through a finiancial institution.



On 9 January 2009, Satoshi released the bitcoin software version 0.1 on Sourceforge, and launched the network by mining the genesis block of bitcoin, whose embedded coinbase transaction's text read: ”The Times 03/Jan/2009 Chancellor on brink of second bailout for banks”. This is a reference to The Times journal's headline published on that date. This quotation, has been interpreted as both a timestamp of the genesis date and a derisive comment on the instability caused by fractional-reserve banking.



Satoshi Nakamoto continued working on his software with the help of ohter developers until mid-2010, when he handed over the source code repository and the network alert key to Gavin anderson and transferred several related domanis to other competent members of the bitcoin community. From this moment forward the creator of bitcoins, stopped being involved in his project except fot test transactions. The ammount of bitcoins owned by Satoshi remain unspent untill this day, and considering the economical peak of bitcoins of december 2017, these would have been worth over 19 billion USD, making Nakamoto the 44th richest person in the world at the time.
