"If you have perfect virtual reality eventually, where you're able to simulate everything that a human can experience or imagine experiencing, it's hard to imagine where you go from there."

—Palmer Luckey
2014

Palmer Luckey
Luckey was frustrated with the quality and efficiency of existing head-mounted displays in the market, so he started experimenting with his own designs in 2009. His first prototype was completed in his parents’ garage in 2010, featuring a 90-degree field of view and low latency. He continued to develop prototypes in the following years, and in 2012 he launched a Kickstarter campaign for the Rift, a prototype he started developing after funding his own company, Oculus VR, two months prior.

The Rift featured a set of lenses that created a stereoscopic 3D image so that each eye sees everything from a slightly different vantage point, creating the optical illusion of depth. It was embedded with sensors that monitor the user’s head motions to adjust the image and has external sensors to track head movement more accurately. A dial within the headset also allows users to adjust the lenses to suit their face. Furthermore, intensive work went into reducing movement latency as much as possible After a number of changes and revisions (dubbed Dev Kits), the Rift was finally released to the public at a much more affordable price than other headsets of the past.
