Linux Today
Despite the fact that Linux was originally developed for PCs on the Intel x86 architecture, its success is principally due to its usage on a wide variety of platforms.

Servers

Linux was developed at about the same time of the web and offered a perfect solution for installing web servers. These systems only need to efficiently implement a small set of feature (internet connection and reading files, no window systems) and Linux was immediately usable for that for free.

Now over 96.4% of the top 1 million web servers' operating systems are Linux.

Supercomputers

Historically, supercomputers were very expensive and very specialized machines. Starting from the middle of the 90’s several research centers experimented with creating a supercomputer by assembling many commodity PCs.
Linux was a perfect solution because it was very efficient in communication, open-source and cheap. Since it was open-source Linux could be modified for creating computer clusters very quickly.
Nowadays it is the only OS used on TOP500 supercomputers.

Mobiles

Thanks to its versatility Linux was adapted to smartphones. In particular Android, which is based on a modified version of the Linux kernel, is the most diffused mobile operating system.
In 2019 Android was installed on more than 2.5 billion devices; for reference at the same date Microsoft Windows was used on 1.5 billion PCs. In October 2020, 73% of smartphones in the world are Linux-based.

Embedded systems

Due to its low cost and its versatility Linux is also used in computers with dedicated functions, such as routers, televisions, cars controllers…
This was made possible by the porting of the Linux kernel to many CPUs not only used in PCs but very popular for embedded devices (for example ARM and ARC).

Desktop and laptop

This was the original platform for Linux and it’s probably the less successful in terms of market share. Nowadays only about 2% of the desktop and laptop PCs are running Linux. The platform is fully functional with many distributions, windows systems, graphics support, applications…
The vast majority of PCs are sold with a commercial OS already installed (Windows or macOS).
