3D graphics in this decade


The decade of the 90s has been characterized by important innovations in terms of hardware and software in the field of digital graphics.
Today the latter has reached such an advanced level that we can recreate in the form of images practically anything conceivable by the human mind. A perfect example of this idea are movies, in which we are accustomed to see scenes that, if we think about it for a moment, would actually be impossible to realize in reality because they are set in places that are impossible for man to reach; like the inside of a black hole in Christopher Nolan's Interstellar (definitely to put on the list in case you have never seen it ;) or that don't even exist because they're completely the result of imagination like the moon "Pandora" in James Cameron's Avatar.
All this is made possible by 3D computer graphics technology that laid its foundations during the 90s.



The role of NVIDIA and Jen-Hsun Huang


If we want to choose a person and a company that played a fundamental and decisive role in the development of digital graphics during the 90s (and beyond) we can do nothing other than mention Jen-Hsun Huang, current CEO and co-founder of NVIDIA corporation or, if we want to put it another way, CEO of one of the two titans of the graphics card market along with Advanced Micro Devices (AMD).

Quite comically, Mr. Huang, before taking the path that would lead him to become the 61st highest-paid CEO in the United States (according to a 2008 Forbes ranking), worked as a microprocessor designer for the same AMD he would declare war on a few years later.
However, he didn't like the idea of a mediocre life limited to listening and suffering the decisions of others. So it was then that in 1993 in Santa Clara, California, Jen-Hsun Huang founds NVIDIA with Chris Malachowsky and Curtis Priem.

The real turning point came a few years later, in 1999, when the company presented to the world a product that would change the history of computer graphics forever: the GeForce 256, the first and historical version of the famous GeForce GPU series that will be the specific topic of the next two pages.

In the quote above, NVIDIA's CEO speaks about this product, calling it a "groundbreaking technology" and also "trend-setting product to the PC market". In fact, Jen-Hsun Huang, saying this, was not exaggerating at all because the GeForce 256 was a great revolution in computer graphics for its time. It's no coincidence that NVIDIA marketing executives at the time decided to advertise it as "the world's first GPU".
